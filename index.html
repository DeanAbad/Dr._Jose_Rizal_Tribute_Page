Dr. Jose Rizal

The Filipino who fought for freedom with his pen.

1861

On 19th of June, Jose Rizal was born in Calamba, Laguna. He is the 7th child of Francisco Rizal Mercado his father, and Teodora Alonso Realonda his mother. After three days he was baptized under the name of Jose Protacio Rizal-Mercado y Alonzo-Realonda.

1870

Rizal starts school under supervision of Justiniano Aquin Cruz when he was 9 years old.

1871

Rizal continues education under the guidance of Lucas Padua.

1872

Rizal was examined at the entrance of St. Tomas University in Manila, and he enters the school as a scholar.

1875

Rizal enters the Ateneo de Manila University as a boarder at 14 years of age.

1876

Rizal receives Bachelor of Arts Degree from the Ateneo de Manila University at the age of 15. He received it with highest honors.

1877

In June, Rizal studies philosophy at St. Tomas University in Manila.

1877

In November, Rizal wrote a poem and received a recognition from the Royal Economic Society of Friends i.e. Amigos del Pals.

1878

Rizal transfers into the medical courses at St. Tomas University. He wrote two poems that won him other recognitions.

1880

Rizal wrote another poem for a competition, but he was not given the first prize due to discrimination. In December, he produced his first Operetta called On the Banks of the Pasig.

1881

Rizal creates the commemorative medal in wax for the Royal Economic Society of Friends centennial celebration.

1882

In May, Rizal got oney from his brother and travelled secretly from Manila to Spain aboard a French ship, and railroad entering Spain at the Port Bou. In June, St. Tomas University realizes Rizal is nowhere to be found and threatens to take away their land. In June, he arrived in Barcelona and begins to study again in Madrid in October.

1886

Rizal receives a degree in medicine from the Central Univerrsity of Madrid at the age of 23. He became an assistant to Dr. L. de Wecker and visited universities in Berlin, Leipzig, and Heidelberg in Germany.

1887

Rizal finished his novel Noli Me Tangere while in Berlin. The novel offended several Catholic officials and thus he was deemed as a troublemaker. He travelled to Austria, Italy, and Switzerland. On 3rd of July, he left from Mersailles, Italy and arrived in Manila on August 5th. He travelled to areas escorted by a Spanish Lieutenant.

1892

In August, Rizal was detained aboard a ship travelling back to Spain. He was charged with treason, sedition, and the formation of illegal societies. He was sent to an island and held. He taught for 4 years while being held. He met Jospahine Bracken who he wanted to marry but the church refused to provide him a license for it. He penned his last piece of writing called My Last Farewell and wrote an address to Filipino insurgents to lay down their arms against the Spanish. It never made out to the public, and was only added as charges by the Spaniards against Rizal.

1896

Rizal's request to go to Cuba was approved. However, on the way to Cuba he was arrested and charged again with conspiracy and sedition. On 27th of December, he was condemned to death in a Spanish court. On 30th of the same month, he was married to Josephine Bracken, and after 2 hours he was executed by a firing squad composed of fellow Filipino men.

1912

On the 30th of December, Rizal's ashes were transferred to the Rizal Mausoleum and that day was declared as a national holiday to honor his memory. Jose Rizal's works and life were crucial to Filipinos who were yearning from freedom against the Spanish colonization. He died at age of 35.
